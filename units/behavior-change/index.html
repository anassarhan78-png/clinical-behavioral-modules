🧠 What is Behavior Change? (Audio)
Definition: "Deliberate and systematic efforts to alter one or more behavioral patterns" with the goal of improving health and well-being. Stages: Awareness → Contemplation → Preparation → Action → Maintenance. Effective behavior change requires self-motivation, support, and persistence.
الترجمة:
"جهود مقصودة ومنهجية لتعديل نمط سلوكي واحد أو أكثر" بهدف تحسين الصحة والعافية. المراحل: الوعي → التفكير → التجهيز → الفعل → المحافظة. يتطلب تغيير السلوك الفعال دافعاً ذاتياً ودعماً ومثابرة.

📝 Essay Questions & Model Answers (Detailed – 20 Questions)

Click on each question to see the model answer, including why some answers are wrong and why the correct answer is right.

1️⃣ Analyze Mrs. Noor's perceived barriers using HBM. How can the physician address each barrier? Provide evidence-based strategies.
✅ Correct Answer: Mrs. Noor has barriers: (a) Lack of knowledge about nutrition/exercise → provide simple diet plan (plate method) and prescribe daily walking. (b) Difficulty changing habits → motivational interviewing and small steps. (c) Lack of time → 10-min micro-workouts, meal prep. (d) Low self-efficacy → graded tasks and verbal persuasion. ❌ Common mistake: Telling the patient "just lose weight" without exploring barriers leads to failure. Ignoring family responsibilities is another error.
2️⃣ Differentiate between perceived susceptibility and perceived severity. Use direct quotes from Mrs. Noor. Why are both necessary?
✅ Susceptibility: "I feel breathless while climbing stairs, so I feel I'm at risk." ✅ Severity: "It's difficult to control symptoms and high risk of complications. It also impacts work and family life." Both are needed because: if only susceptibility is high but severity low, patient may ignore; if severity high but susceptibility low, they think "it won't happen to me". HBM requires both for threat perception.
3️⃣ Describe three evidence-based techniques to improve Mrs. Noor's self-efficacy (slide 19). Explain why each works.
✅ 1. Personal accomplishment: Set tiny goals (e.g., walk 5 min/day) – success builds confidence. ✅ 2. Vicarious experience: Share stories of similar patients who lost weight – seeing peers succeed raises belief. ✅ 3. Verbal persuasion: Doctor says "You already reduced sugar, I'm confident you can do more" – reduces self-doubt.
4️⃣ Explain the role of cues to action in HBM. Give three examples from the case and suggest additional cues the doctor can introduce.
✅ Cues trigger action. Existing: doctor's advice, friend's health status, TV programs. New: written action plan, text reminders, placing walking shoes by door.
5️⃣ How would you apply the Transtheoretical Model (stages of change) to Mrs. Noor? Detail each stage.
Noor is in contemplation/preparation. Precontemplation (denial) not present. Contemplation: aware of risks but ambivalent. Preparation: small steps. Action: start walking. Maintenance: follow-ups.
6️⃣ What communication skills are essential for the physician? (Slide 12) Apply each to Noor.
Open-ended questions, empathy, active listening, summarizing, clarification, eye contact. Example: "What concerns you most about your weight?"
7️⃣ Compare perceived benefits and perceived barriers. Why must benefits outweigh barriers for behavior change?
Benefits: reduced complications, better heart health. Barriers: time, knowledge. If barriers are higher, no action. Physician must reduce barriers.
8️⃣ Describe a 4-week action plan for Mrs. Noor. Include diet, exercise, and psychological support.
Week 1: education, 5-min walks. Week 2: 10-min walks, remove sugary drinks. Week 3: add strength exercises, refer to dietitian. Week 4: review progress, set new goals.
9️⃣ How does stress affect Mrs. Noor's diabetes and heart disease? Integrate psychosocial factors.
Stress increases cortisol, raises blood sugar and blood pressure, worsens heart disease. Needs relaxation techniques and social support.
🔟 What is the difference between "cue to action" and "self-efficacy"? Give original examples.
Cue is external trigger (doctor's advice). Self-efficacy is internal confidence (belief in ability). Both needed.
1️⃣1️⃣ Discuss the role of family support in Noor's weight loss journey. How can the physician involve family?
Family can help with meal prep, encouragement, and reducing stress. Doctor can invite family to sessions.
1️⃣2️⃣ Why is shared decision making important (slide 22)? Apply to Noor.
Increases adherence, respects patient autonomy. Ask Noor what goals she prefers.
1️⃣3️⃣ How would you handle a relapse (e.g., Noor stops exercise after 2 weeks)?
Normalize relapse, identify barriers, adjust plan, reinforce self-efficacy.
1️⃣4️⃣ Compare HBM with Theory of Planned Behavior. Which is more suitable for Noor?
HBM focuses on perceived threat and benefits; TPB adds subjective norms and perceived control. Both useful.
1️⃣5️⃣ Write a script for a physician using motivational interviewing with Noor.
"On a scale 1-10, how important is weight loss? Why not lower?" etc.
1️⃣6️⃣ What are the dangers of stopping diabetes medication? How does it relate to Noor's case?
Hyperglycemia, complications. Noor hasn't stopped but may if frustrated.
1️⃣7️⃣ Explain the concept of "catastrophic thinking" in the context of Noor's fear of heart attack.
She may think "every chest pain means heart attack". Need cognitive restructuring.
1️⃣8️⃣ How can technology (apps, trackers) help Noor? Give specific examples.
Step counter, calorie tracker, reminder apps, online support groups.
1️⃣9️⃣ What are the ethical considerations when a patient denies the severity of obesity?
Respect autonomy, provide evidence without shaming, use shared decision making.
2️⃣0️⃣ Propose a long-term maintenance plan for Noor after she loses weight.
Monthly follow-up, support group, gradual increase in activity, relapse prevention strategies.
